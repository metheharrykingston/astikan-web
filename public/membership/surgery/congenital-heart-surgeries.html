Understanding Congenital Heart Surgery

Congenital heart surgery (a procedure to correct heart defects present from birth) helps your child's heart work normally and improves long-term health. Our specialized team performs congenital heart disease surgery in Delhi NCR to repair structural heart problems, enhance blood circulation, and restore oxygen flow — helping patients live active, healthy lives.

When Is Congenital Heart Surgery Indicated?

  • Ventricular Septal Defects (holes between heart chambers)
  • Tetralogy of Fallot and other complex cyanotic defects
  • Transposition of the Great Arteries (TGA)
  • Hypoplastic Left Heart Syndrome (HLHS)
  • Coarctation of the Aorta and significant valve abnormalities

Each procedure is tailored to patient's anatomy and health condition to ensure safety, accuracy, and lasting results at our state-of-the-art facilities across Delhi, Noida, Gurgaon, and other NCR regions.

Septal Defect Repair

Our surgeons repair heart walls safely to restore healthy blood flow and help your child live a normal life. This surgery closes atrial or ventricular septal defects (holes in heart's walls) to separate chambers properly and prevent oxygen-poor blood from mixing with oxygen-rich blood.

Complex Reconstruction

Advanced surgical reconstruction for complex congenital anomalies, including single-ventricle physiology and multi-stage heart repairs, performed with the highest safety standards at our Delhi NCR hospitals.

Optimized Heart Function

Each surgery aims to restore normal circulation and oxygen delivery, supporting better cardiac performance and overall growth for patients from Delhi, Noida, Gurgaon, and surrounding areas.

Congenital Heart Surgery Procedures

Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD) Repair

Surgical closure of holes in the heart's ventricular septum using patches or direct suturing techniques.

Duration: 2-4 hours
Recovery: 4-6 weeks
Key Benefits:
  • Normalizes blood flow between chambers
  • Prevents pulmonary hypertension
  • Improves long-term cardiac function
Best For:

Symptomatic or large VSDs causing complications

Tetralogy of Fallot Repair

Complete repair addressing four heart defects: VSD closure, pulmonary valve relief, and right ventricular reconstruction.

Duration: 4-6 hours
Recovery: 6-8 weeks
Key Benefits:
  • Corrects multiple defects simultaneously
  • Restores normal oxygenation
  • Supports normal physical activity
Best For:

Patients with complex Tetralogy of Fallot

Fontan Procedure

Staged palliation for single ventricle conditions, directing venous blood directly to the lungs.

Duration: 4-8 hours
Recovery: 8-12 weeks
Key Benefits:
  • Improves oxygenation
  • Reduces cardiac workload
  • Enhances overall quality of life
Best For:

Children with Hypoplastic Left Heart Syndrome (HLHS) or single ventricle conditions

Warning Signs

Contact your doctor immediately if you notice any of the following warning signs:

  • Difficulty breathing or feeding
  • Fever or signs of infection
  • Changes in activity or behavior
  • Unusual fatigue or irritability
