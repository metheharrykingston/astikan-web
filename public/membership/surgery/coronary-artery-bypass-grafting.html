Understanding Heart Bypass (CABG) Surgery

Heart bypass surgery (CABG) helps restore blood flow to your heart when arteries are blocked — improving heart function, breathing, and overall quality of life. CABG surgery means creating a new pathway for blood to flow to the heart muscle, bypassing narrowed or clogged arteries. This is one of the most common and trusted open heart bypass surgeries performed worldwide at our state-of-the-art facilities in Delhi NCR.

When Is CABG Surgery Recommended?

  • Multiple coronary blockages (70% or more)
  • Left main coronary artery disease with major narrowing
  • Failed angioplasty or stent procedures
  • Unstable angina (chest pain at rest not relieved by medication)
  • Heart attack with ongoing complications or risk of recurrence
Improved Heart Function

CABG enhances heart pumping efficiency, easing chest pain, breathlessness, and fatigue.

Minimally Invasive Options

Minimally invasive CABG surgery offers smaller cuts, faster healing, and less discomfort — with the same lasting results.

Symptom Relief & Quality of Life

Regain stamina, enjoy daily activities without pain, and feel renewed confidence in your heart's health.

Long-Term Protection

CABG surgery provides durable relief from coronary artery disease and lowers your risk of future heart attacks.

Types of Bypass Procedures

Depending on your condition, your doctor will choose the safest and most effective approach for you. CABG surgery procedure options include:
Traditional Open-Heart Bypass

The gold-standard approach performed through a full chest incision using a heart-lung machine.

Duration: 3–6 hours
Recovery: 6–8 weeks
Key Benefits:
  • Highest success rate for complex cases
  • Suitable for multiple vessel disease
Best For:

Multiple vessel disease, complex blockages

Minimally Invasive Bypass

Performed through smaller incisions between the ribs, often without stopping the heart.

Duration: 2–4 hours
Recovery: 3–4 weeks
Key Benefits:
  • Smaller cuts, faster healing
  • Less discomfort with lasting results
Best For:

Single or double vessel disease

Off-Pump Bypass

Performed on a beating heart without using a heart-lung machine.

Duration: 2–5 hours
Recovery: 4–6 weeks
Key Benefits:
  • Reduced stroke risk
  • Lower chance of kidney complications
  • Faster recovery
Best For:

High-risk patients or those with kidney disease

Recovery Guidelines & Support

Physical Care
  • Take medicines on time and start walking a little every day — it helps your heart heal faster.
  • Keep incision sites clean and dry
  • Avoid heavy lifting for 6–8 weeks
Lifestyle Changes
  • Small daily changes like eating light and managing stress can protect your heart for years to come.
  • Follow a heart-healthy diet
  • Quit smoking completely
  • Practice stress-relief and relaxation techniques
Warning Signs
  • If you experience any of the following after surgery, contact your doctor immediately:
  • Chest pain or tightness
  • Shortness of breath at rest
  • Signs of infection (redness, fever, or discharge)
  • Irregular heartbeat or palpitations
