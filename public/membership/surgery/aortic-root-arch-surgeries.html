Understanding Aortic Root & Arch Surgery at Astikan Health

The aorta is your body's main artery, carrying oxygen-rich blood from the heart to every part of you. When the aortic root diameter becomes enlarged (a condition known as aortic root dilation) or develops an aortic root aneurysm, or when the aortic arch is affected, urgent steps may be required. These renewed protection and peace of mind.

When Is Aortic Root & Arch Surgery Recommended?

  • A significant aortic root aneurysm of the root or arch with high risk of rupture
  • A tear in the aortic wall (aortic dissection) needing urgent repair
  • Severe aortic valve disease alongside enlargement of the aortic root
  • Genetic connective tissue disorders (such as Marfan syndrome) or bicuspid aortic valve causing aortic root dilation
  • Complex abnormalities in the aortic arch affecting blood flow to your brain and upper body

Aortic Root Reconstruction

In this procedure at our Noida facility, the weakened segment of the aortic root is either repaired or fully replaced. We tailor the technique to your unique anatomy and condition of your aortic root normal size versus its challenged state. By restoring the root to a safe dimension, we help safeguard your valve and improve how your heart works.

Advanced Aortic Arch Repair

When the disease involves the aortic arch, a more complex intervention is needed to reconstruct the arch, maintain blood flow to critical organs including the brain, and protect your future wellness. At Astikan Health in Noida, we employ sophisticated methods like hypothermic circulatory arrest and advanced neuromonitoring, to ensure your safety throughout.

Restored Blood Flow

Aortic Root & Arch Procedures at Astikan Health, Noida

Our cardiothoracic team at our Noida, Delhi NCR facility performs highly specialized aortic surgeries tailored to your individual anatomy and condition. Using evidence-based techniques, we determine the safest and most effective surgical approach for your aortic root and arch pathology.

Bentall Procedure

A time-tested method where the entire aortic root is replaced with a composite valve-conduit graft covering both aneurysm of the root and valve disease in one go. This advanced procedure is performed by our expert cardiac surgeons at our Noida facility.

Duration: 4-6 hours
Recovery: 8-12 weeks
Key Benefits:
  • Simultaneous treatment of aortic root aneurysm and valve pathology
  • Durable long-term outcomes
  • Single-stage complete repair
Best For:

Patients with aortic root aneurysms involving the valve

Valve-Sparing Root Replacement

In cases where your aortic valve is still functioning well, we can preserve it while replacing the dilated aortic root. Techniques like the David or Yacoub method at our Noida facility allow you to keep your native valve and avoid lifelong anticoagulation.

Duration: 5-7 hours
Recovery: 8-10 weeks
Key Benefits:
  • Preserves the patient's natural aortic valve
  • No lifelong anticoagulation required
  • Excellent long-term hemodynamics
Best For:

Root aneurysms with normally functioning aortic valves

Total Arch Replacement

For extensive disease of the aortic arch, we perform a comprehensive replacement at our Delhi NCR facility, protecting cerebral perfusion and ensuring your upper body continues to receive healthy blood flow.

Duration: 6-10 hours
Recovery: 10-16 weeks
Key Benefits:
  • Treats extensive aortic arch pathology
  • Protects cerebral and organ perfusion
  • Prevents future life-threatening complications
Best For:

Extensive aortic arch aneurysms or dissections

Your Recovery Journey at Astikan Health, Noida

Recovery after aortic root and arch surgery at our Noida, Delhi NCR facility requires careful monitoring and structured rehabilitation. Our multidisciplinary cardiac team provides comprehensive guidance and support at every stage of healing.

Intensive Care Unit (ICU)

Initial 5-10 days

Continuous hemodynamic and neurological monitoring

  • Neurological status assessment
  • Ventilator support and gradual weaning
  • Management of chest drains and lines
  • Early mobilization under supervision

Step-Down / Hospital Recovery

2-3 weeks

Progressive recovery with ongoing monitoring

  • Gradual mobilization and supervised walking
  • Wound and sternal healing assessment
  • Medication adjustment including anticoagulation
  • Echocardiography and imaging follow-up

Home Recovery

6-12 weeks

Supervised home rehabilitation and gradual return to activity

  • Activity restrictions: avoid lifting heavy objects
  • Structured walking and physical therapy program
  • Regular follow-up appointments with cardiac team
  • Medication adherence and lifestyle guidance

Long-Term Recovery

3-6 months

Return to normal activities with ongoing cardiac care

  • Gradual resumption of work and daily activities
  • Full clearance for physical activity under medical supervision
  • Periodic imaging and echocardiography to monitor aorta and valve
  • Ongoing cardiac risk factor management

Recovery Guidelines & Support at Astikan Health, Noida

Critical Care Considerations

  • Strict adherence to prescribed medications
  • Monitor for neurological or cardiac symptoms
  • Allow adequate sternum and incision healing
  • Blood pressure and heart rhythm monitoring

Activity Guidelines

  • Avoid heavy lifting for at least 3 months
  • Delay driving until medically cleared
  • No strenuous exercise until fully cleared
  • Follow prescribed cardiac rehabilitation protocol

Emergency Warning Signs

  • Sudden severe chest, back, or abdominal pain
  • Confusion, dizziness, or neurological changes
  • Fever, redness, or signs of infection at incision site
  • Shortness of breath or palpitations
