Understanding Varicose Vein Treatment

Varicose veins are enlarged, twisted veins that usually appear in the legs. They can cause aching, heaviness, swelling, and sometimes skin changes.
treatments focused on improving circulation, reducing discomfort, and addressing cosmetic concerns with safe, personalized care.

When Is Varicose Vein Treatment Recommended?

  • Visible bulging or twisted veins in the legs or thighs
  • Leg pain, heaviness, or throbbing after prolonged standing or sitting
  • Swelling or skin discoloration around affected veins
  • Persistent itching, burning, or irritation over veins
  • Complications such as venous ulcers or bleeding
Symptom Relief

Treatments help reduce leg pain, swelling, and fatigue, improving comfort and mobility in daily life.

Minimally Invasive Care

Options like laser therapy, radiofrequency ablation, and sclerotherapy are performed safely without major surgery.

Faster Recovery

Most procedures are outpatient with minimal downtime, allowing a quicker return to normal activities.

Improved Appearance

Treatments help reduce the visibility of varicose veins and support healthier-looking legs and ankles.

Endovenous Laser Treatment (EVLT)

A minimally invasive procedure using laser energy to close affected veins from within, restoring normal blood flow.

Duration: 30-60 minutes
Recovery: 2-3 days
Key Benefits:
  • No surgical incision required
  • Quick return to daily activities
  • Minimal discomfort post-procedure
Best For:

Moderate to large varicose veins

Sclerotherapy

A specialized chemical solution is injected into smaller veins, causing them to collapse and gradually fade.

Duration: 20-30 minutes
Recovery: 1-2 days
Key Benefits:
  • No anesthesia required
  • Ideal for spider veins and mild varicose veins
  • Outpatient procedure with minimal disruption
Best For:

Spider veins and small superficial veins

Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA)

Heat generated by radiofrequency energy seals the faulty vein, rerouting blood through healthy vessels.

Duration: 30-45 minutes
Recovery: 2-4 days
Key Benefits:
  • Minimally invasive with local anesthesia
  • Less bruising and pain than traditional surgery
  • Effective for larger or symptomatic veins
Best For:

Large, symptomatic varicose veins

Your Recovery Journey

Recovery from varicose vein treatment is usually fast and comfortable. Our care team will
guide you through every phase to ensure smooth healing and long-lasting results.
Post-Treatment Observation
0–1 day

Initial monitoring and rest

  • Outpatient procedure, minimal hospital time
  • Wear compression stockings immediately
  • Light walking encouraged within a few hours
  • Minor discomfort is normal
Early Recovery
1–7 days

Healing at home with mild activity

  • Continue wearing compression stockings
  • Avoid long periods of standing or sitting
  • Minor bruising and swelling is normal
  • Most patients resume light work in 2–3 days
Active Recovery
1–3 weeks

Gradually resuming regular routine

  • Increase walking and light exercises
  • Swelling reduces gradually
  • Attend follow-up ultrasound scans
  • Avoid strenuous activities until cleared
Long-Term Results
4+ weeks

Full healing and cosmetic improvement

  • Veins fade and skin appearance improves
  • Discomfort is fully resolved
  • Maintain an active lifestyle
  • Prevent recurrence with self-care

Recovery Guidelines & Support

Physical Care
  • Wear compression stockings daily
  • Keep the treated area clean and dry
  • Take short walks regularly
  • Avoid heavy lifting for 1–2 weeks
Lifestyle Changes
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Avoid prolonged sitting or standing
  • Stay physically active daily
  • Drink plenty of water
When to Seek Help
  • Sudden leg swelling or severe pain
  • Signs of infection (redness, pus, fever)
  • Bleeding that doesn’t stop
  • Numbness or tingling in legs

Common Questions

Minimally invasive treatments typically take 30–60 minutes per session, depending on the number of veins and the technique used. Procedures like laser ablation or sclerotherapy are usually outpatient.

Risks are generally low but may include bruising, swelling, or temporary nerve irritation. Rare complications like blood clots or skin discoloration can occur.

Most patients resume normal activities within 24 hours. Strenuous activity should be avoided for a few days. Compression stockings may be recommended to support healing.

Many patients return to work within a day or two, especially after non-surgical treatments. Jobs involving heavy lifting or prolonged standing may require additional recovery time. Your doctor will provide personalized guidance.

Usually, no long-term medications are needed. Mild pain relievers may be used temporarily. Occasionally, short-term medications to prevent clotting are advised.

Modern treatments like laser ablation, radiofrequency, and sclerotherapy are highly effective, with success rates above 90%. Most patients experience symptom relief and cosmetic improvement. Long-term results improve with follow-ups and lifestyle changes.

Treated veins rarely return, but new veins may develop over time. Regular follow-ups, wearing compression stockings, and maintaining a healthy lifestyle help reduce recurrence.

Stay active with regular walking, avoid prolonged sitting or standing, maintain a healthy weight, wear compression stockings as advised, and elevate legs when possible. These habits support vein health and prevent recurrence.
