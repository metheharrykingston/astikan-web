Understanding Your Condition

What is Peripheral Artery Disease?

Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD) occurs when plaque builds up in arteries, narrowing them and reducing blood flow to the legs or arms, causing pain or wounds. Peripheral endarterectomy removes this plaque to restore circulation and improve symptoms.

The Concern

Untreated PAD can lead to severe leg pain, non-healing ulcers, or amputation in up to 5% of advanced cases.

Our Approach

We perform precise endarterectomy to remove plaque, tailored to your specific arterial blockages.

Your Results

With a 92% success rate, our treatments relieve pain, restore blood flow, and enhance mobility.

Important Warning Signs

Spot PAD Signs Early

Early detection of peripheral artery disease can prevent complications like limb loss. If you notice these symptoms, contact us immediately for expert evaluation and care.

Seek Help
Leg Pain While Walking

Cramping or pain in the legs during activity (claudication) that eases with rest.

Seek Help
Non-Healing Wounds

Sores or ulcers on the legs or feet that heal slowly or not at all.

Seek Help
Cold Limbs

Coldness in the legs or feet, especially compared to the other side.

Skin Changes

Shiny, hairless skin or discoloration in the legs or feet.

Weak Pulse

Weak or absent pulse in the legs or feet, indicating reduced blood flow.

Seek Help
Severe Leg Pain at Rest

Persistent leg pain at rest, signaling a critical arterial blockage.

Remember P.A.I.N. for PAD Awareness

P

Pain while walking

A

Altered skin appearance

I

Impaired wound healing

N

Need urgent care

Advanced Peripheral Endarterectomy Treatments

At Astikan Health, our expert vascular team performs peripheral endarterectomy to remove plaque from blocked arteries, restoring blood flow and relieving symptoms of peripheral artery disease.

90-94% Success

Femoral Endarterectomy

A surgical procedure to remove plaque from the femoral artery in the thigh, restoring blood flow to the lower leg.

Time: 1–2 hours
Recovery: 2–4 weeks
  • Clears thigh artery blockages
  • Relieves claudication pain
  • Improves leg circulation
Ideal For:
Localized femoral artery blockages causing leg pain
88-92% Success

Iliac Endarterectomy

A procedure to remove plaque from the iliac arteries in the pelvis, improving blood flow to the legs.

Time: 2–3 hours
Recovery: 3–5 weeks
  • Restores pelvic artery flow
  • Reduces severe PAD symptoms
  • Prevents limb complications
Ideal For:
Iliac artery blockages causing leg pain or weakness
85-90% Success

Tibial Endarterectomy

A surgery to clear plaque from the tibial arteries in the lower leg, improving circulation to the feet.

Time: 1–2 hours
Recovery: 3–5 weeks
  • Improves foot blood flow
  • Promotes wound healing
  • Reduces amputation risk
Ideal For:
Severe PAD with foot ulcers or critical ischemia

Peripheral Endarterectomy Recovery Timeline

Recovery from peripheral endarterectomy is manageable, with most patients resuming normal activities in 2–5 weeks with proper care and follow-up.

Right After Procedure

1–2 days
  • Vital signs monitoring
  • Incision site checks
  • Pain management
  • Early mobility encouragement

In-Hospital Healing

2–4 days
  • Wound care
  • Anticoagulant therapy
  • Light walking support
  • Blood flow monitoring

Early At-Home Phase

1–3 weeks
  • Follow-up imaging
  • Gradual activity increase
  • Wound care continuation
  • Medication adherence

Complete Recovery

2–5 weeks
  • Resume normal activities
  • Driving approval
  • Lifestyle changes for PAD
  • Regular vascular checkups
