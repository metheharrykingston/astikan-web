Understanding the Condition

What is Coronary Artery Disease?

Coronary artery disease occurs when plaque builds up in the arteries supplying blood to the heart, causing narrowing or blockages. This can lead to chest pain (angina) or increase the risk of heart attack.

The Problem

Narrowed coronary arteries reduce blood flow to the heart, increasing the risk of heart attack by up to 4x.

The Solution

Angioplasty and stent placement open blocked arteries, restoring blood flow and reducing heart attack risk.

The Outcome

95% success rate in improving heart function and enhancing quality of life for patients with coronary artery disease.

Critical Warning Signs

Recognize Heart Issues Early

Early detection of coronary artery disease can prevent heart attack. Seek immediate medical attention if you experience these symptoms.

Urgent
Chest Pain

Pressure, tightness, or pain in the chest, often spreading to the arms or jaw.

Urgent
Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing during activity or at rest.

Urgent
Cold Sweats

Sudden sweating, often with nausea or lightheadedness.

Fatigue

Unusual tiredness or weakness, especially during physical activity.

Irregular Heartbeat

Palpitations or a feeling of skipped heartbeats.

Urgent
Arm/Shoulder Pain

Pain or discomfort in one or both arms, shoulders, or back.

Our Specialties

Vascular Surgery Procedures

Our expert vascular team specializes in angioplasty and stent placement to treat coronary artery disease and improve heart health. We offer minimally invasive options for faster recovery and better outcomes.

95-97% Success

Coronary Angioplasty

A minimally invasive procedure using a balloon to open narrowed coronary arteries, restoring blood flow to the heart.

Duration: 1–2 hours
Recovery: 1–2 weeks
  • Relieves chest pain
  • Reduces heart attack risk
  • Minimally invasive
Best For:
Patients with significant coronary artery blockages
92-95% Success

Stent Placement

Insertion of a small mesh tube (stent) to keep coronary arteries open, ensuring consistent blood flow to the heart.

Duration: 1–2 hours
Recovery: 1–2 weeks
  • Prevents artery re-narrowing
  • Quick recovery time
  • Long-term heart health
Best For:
Patients with coronary artery disease or acute blockages
90-93% Success

Drug-Eluting Stent Placement

Placement of a stent coated with medication to prevent scar tissue growth, ensuring long-term artery patency.

Duration: 1–2 hours
Recovery: 1–2 weeks
  • Reduces re-blockage risk
  • Enhanced long-term outcomes
  • Minimally invasive
Best For:
Patients with high risk of artery re-narrowing

Angioplasty & Stent Recovery Timeline

Recovery from angioplasty and stent placement is typically quick, with most patients resuming normal activities within 1–2 weeks with proper care and follow-up.

Immediate Post-Op

1–2 days
  • Vital signs monitoring
  • Catheter site checks
  • Pain management
  • Early mobilization

Hospital Recovery

1–2 days
  • Site monitoring for bleeding
  • Heart rhythm checks
  • Light activity encouragement
  • Medication education

Early Home Recovery

3–7 days
  • Follow-up appointments
  • Gradual activity increase
  • Avoid heavy lifting
  • Antiplatelet therapy

Full Recovery

1–2 weeks
  • Resume normal activities
  • Cardiac rehab if needed
  • Follow-up heart scans
  • Long-term heart health plan
