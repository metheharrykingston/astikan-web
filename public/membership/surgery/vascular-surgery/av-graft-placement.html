Understanding Your Needs

What is AV Graft Placement?

AV graft placement is a surgical procedure that creates a reliable access point for dialysis by connecting an artery to a vein using a synthetic tube. This helps ensure effective dialysis for kidney patients, improving treatment outcomes and quality of life.

The Concern

Dialysis patients need strong, dependable vascular access. Without it, treatments can be less effective, impacting health and comfort.

Our Approach

Our skilled surgeons perform AV graft placement with precision, using advanced techniques to ensure safe and durable access tailored to your needs.

Your Results

With a 90% success rate, our AV grafts provide reliable dialysis access, supporting better health and a more active life.

Important Warning Signs

Monitor Your AV Graft Health

Recognizing issues with your AV graft early can ensure effective dialysis and prevent complications. Contact us immediately if you notice these symptoms.

Seek Help
Swelling or Pain

Persistent swelling, redness, or pain around the graft site.

Seek Help
Fever or Infection

Unexplained fever or signs of infection, such as warmth or pus at the graft site.

Seek Help
Weak or No Pulse

Absence of a pulse or thrill (vibration) in the graft during dialysis or checks.

Bleeding at Site

Unexpected bleeding or bruising around the graft area.

Slow Dialysis

Difficulty during dialysis, such as slower flow rates or frequent clotting.

Seek Help
Arm or Hand Issues

Numbness, tingling, or weakness in the arm or hand near the graft.

AV Graft Placement

A surgical procedure to connect an artery to a vein using a synthetic tube, creating a reliable access point for dialysis.

Time: 1–2 hours
Recovery: 2–4 weeks
  • Ensures effective dialysis
  • Durable access solution
  • Reduces dialysis complications
Ideal For:

95-97% Success

AV Fistula Creation

A procedure to directly connect an artery to a vein, creating a natural access point for dialysis with minimal intervention.

Time: 1–2 hours
Recovery: 6–8 weeks
  • Uses your own blood vessels
  • Lower infection risk
  • Long-lasting access
Ideal For:
Patients with suitable veins for long-term dialysis access
85-90% Success

Graft Revision or Repair

A procedure to fix or adjust an existing AV graft to restore proper function and ensure effective dialysis.

Time: 1–3 hours
Recovery: 1–3 weeks
  • Restores graft functionality
  • Prevents dialysis interruptions
  • Improves patient comfort
Ideal For:
Patients with graft complications like clotting or narrowing
