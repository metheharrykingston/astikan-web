Understanding Your Condition

What is Lymphedema?

Lymphedema is a chronic condition causing swelling due to a buildup of lymph fluid, often in the arms or legs, resulting from lymphatic system damage or dysfunction. Our surgical solutions help manage symptoms and improve quality of life.

The Concern

Untreated lymphedema can lead to infections, reduced mobility, and discomfort, affecting daily life and emotional well-being.

Our Approach

We offer advanced surgical techniques like lymph node transfer and lymphatic bypass to reduce swelling and restore function.

Your Results

With an 85% success rate, our treatments reduce swelling and enhance comfort, helping you regain confidence.

Important Warning Signs

Spot Lymphedema Signs Early

Early detection of lymphedema can prevent complications and improve outcomes. If you notice these symptoms, contact us for expert evaluation and support.

Seek Help
Persistent Swelling

Swelling in an arm or leg that doesn’t subside, often feeling heavy or tight.

Seek Help
Skin Tightness

Tight or hardened skin in the affected area, sometimes with a shiny appearance.

Seek Help
Recurrent Infections

Frequent skin infections or cellulitis in the swollen limb.

Limited Mobility

Difficulty moving the affected arm or leg due to swelling or stiffness.

Skin Changes

Thickened or textured skin, sometimes with a warty appearance.

Seek Help
Chronic Discomfort

Ongoing aching or heaviness in the affected limb, impacting daily life.

Remember S.W.E.L.L. for Lymphedema Awareness

Advanced Lymphedema Treatments

At Astikan Health, our expert vascular team provides cutting-edge lymphedema surgeries to reduce swelling, improve mobility, and enhance your quality of life with personalized care.

80-85% Success

Lymphaticovenous Anastomosis (LVA)

A microsurgical procedure connecting lymphatic vessels to veins to improve lymph drainage and reduce swelling.

Time: 2–4 hours
Recovery: 2–4 weeks
  • Minimally invasive microsurgery
  • Improves lymph flow
  • Reduces limb swelling
Ideal For:
Early-stage lymphedema with viable lymphatic vessels
85-90% Success

Vascularized Lymph Node Transfer (VLNT)

Transfers healthy lymph nodes to the affected area to restore lymphatic function and reduce swelling.

Time: 4–6 hours
Recovery: 4–6 weeks
  • Restores lymphatic function
  • Reduces chronic swelling
  • Improves quality of life
Ideal For:
Moderate to severe lymphedema
75-80% Success

Debulking Surgery

Removes excess tissue in severe lymphedema cases to reduce limb size and improve mobility.

Time: 3–5 hours
Recovery: 6–8 weeks
  • Reduces limb size
  • Improves mobility
  • Decreases infection risk
Ideal For:
Advanced lymphedema with significant tissue buildup

Lymphedema Surgery Recovery Timeline

Recovery from lymphedema surgery varies by procedure, with most patients seeing improvement in 2–8 weeks with proper care and follow-up.

Right After Procedure

1–3 days
  • Vital signs monitoring
  • Surgical site care
  • Pain management
  • Early compression therapy

In-Hospital Healing

3–7 days
  • Wound monitoring
  • Lymphatic drainage support
  • Light activity guidance
  • Compression garment fitting

Early At-Home Phase

2–4 weeks
  • Follow-up evaluations
  • Gradual activity increase
  • Continued compression therapy
  • Lymphatic massage if advised

Long-Term Recovery

4–8 weeks
  • Resume normal activities
  • Improved mobility
  • Ongoing compression use
  • Regular lymphatic checkups
