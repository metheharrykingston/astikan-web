Understanding Your Needs

What is AV Fistula Creation?

AV fistula creation is a surgical procedure that connects an artery directly to a vein, typically in the arm, to create a robust access point for dialysis. This enhances treatment efficiency and supports long-term health for kidney patients.

The Challenge

Dialysis requires reliable vascular access. Inadequate access can lead to ineffective treatments, increasing complications.

The Solution

AV fistula creation uses your own blood vessels to form a durable, natural access point, ensuring smooth dialysis sessions.

The Outcome

With a 95% success rate, our AV fistulas provide reliable access, improving dialysis outcomes and quality of life.

Critical Warning Signs

Monitor Your AV Fistula Health

Early detection of AV fistula issues can ensure effective dialysis and prevent complications. Contact us immediately if you notice these symptoms.

Urgent
Swelling or Redness

Persistent swelling, redness, or warmth around the fistula site.

Urgent
Fever or Infection

Unexplained fever or signs of infection, such as pus at the fistula site.

Urgent
Weak or Absent Thrill

No vibration (thrill) felt in the fistula, indicating possible blockage.

Prolonged Bleeding

Excessive bleeding or bruising at the fistula site after dialysis.

Arm Discomfort

Pain, numbness, or weakness in the arm with the fistula.

Urgent
Dialysis Issues

Difficulty during dialysis, such as slow flow or frequent clotting.

Follow C.A.R.E. for Fistula Health

C

Clean the site daily

A

Avoid tight clothing

R

Regular check-ups

E

Early reporting of issues

Our Specialties

Dialysis Access Procedures

Our expert vascular team specializes in AV fistula creation and related procedures to ensure reliable dialysis access. We provide safe, effective treatments tailored to your needs for optimal dialysis outcomes.

AV Fistula Creation

A surgical procedure to connect an artery directly to a vein, creating a strong, natural access point for dialysis.

Duration: 1–2 hours
Recovery: 6–8 weeks
  • Uses your own blood vessels
  • Lower risk of infection
  • Long-lasting dialysis access
Best For:
Patients with suitable veins needing long-term dialysis access
90-92% Success

AV Graft Placement

A procedure using a synthetic tube to connect an artery to a vein, providing reliable dialysis access for patients with weaker veins.

Duration: 1–2 hours
Recovery: 2–4 weeks
  • Effective for complex cases
  • Faster usability for dialysis
  • High success rate
Best For:
Patients with unsuitable veins for fistula creation
85-90% Success

Fistula Revision

A procedure to repair or adjust an existing AV fistula to restore proper function and ensure effective dialysis.

Duration: 1–3 hours
Recovery: 1–3 weeks
  • Restores fistula functionality
  • Prevents dialysis interruptions
  • Enhances patient comfort
Best For:
Patients with fistula complications like narrowing or clotting
