Understanding Your Condition

What is Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)?

Deep Vein Thrombosis occurs when a blood clot forms in a deep vein, typically in the legs. If untreated, these clots can travel to the lungs, causing a life-threatening pulmonary embolism. We're here to guide you through effective treatment.

The Concern

Blood clots in deep veins can break loose, leading to pulmonary embolism with a mortality risk of up to 30% if untreated.

Our Approach

We use advanced procedures like thrombectomy and IVC filter placement to remove or prevent clots, tailored to your needs.

Your Results

With a 93% success rate, our treatments prevent complications and support a healthier life.

Important Warning Signs

Spot DVT Signs Early

Early detection of DVT can prevent serious complications like pulmonary embolism. If you notice these symptoms, contact us immediately for support.

Seek Help
Leg Swelling

Swelling in one leg, often accompanied by pain or tenderness.

Seek Help
Leg Pain

Cramping or soreness in the leg, often starting in the calf.

Seek Help
Skin Changes

Red or discolored skin on the affected leg, often warm to the touch.

Shortness of Breath

Sudden difficulty breathing, indicating possible pulmonary embolism.

Chest Pain

Sharp chest pain that worsens with deep breathing.

Seek Help
Rapid Heart Rate

Fast or irregular heartbeat, signaling a possible emergency.

Remember C.L.O.T. for DVT Awareness

C

Clot symptoms (swelling, pain)

L

Leg discomfort

O

Observe skin changes

T

Time to call emergency

Advanced DVT Treatments

At Astikan Health, our caring vascular team provides effective DVT surgery and IVC filter placement to prevent pulmonary embolism and improve your vascular health, tailored to your unique needs.

90-93% Success

Catheter-Directed Thrombolysis

A minimally invasive procedure to dissolve blood clots in deep veins using medication delivered through a catheter.

Time: 1–2 hours
Recovery: 1–2 weeks
  • Directly dissolves clots
  • Reduces risk of complications
  • Faster recovery than surgery
Ideal For:
Acute DVT with significant symptoms
95-97% Success

IVC Filter Placement

A small device placed in the inferior vena cava to catch blood clots before they reach the lungs, preventing pulmonary embolism.

Time: 30–60 minutes
Recovery: 1–2 days
  • Minimally invasive
  • Quick procedure and recovery
  • Effective embolism prevention
Ideal For:
Patients at high risk for pulmonary embolism
88-92% Success

Venous Thrombectomy

Surgical removal of a blood clot from a deep vein to restore blood flow and prevent complications.

Time: 2–3 hours
Recovery: 2–4 weeks
  • Directly removes large clots
  • Restores vein function
  • Prevents long-term damage
Ideal For:
Severe or extensive DVT cases

DVT Treatment Recovery Timeline

Recovery from DVT surgery or IVC filter placement is often quick, with most patients resuming normal activities in 1–2 weeks with proper care and follow-up.

Right After Procedure

1–2 days
  • Vital signs monitoring
  • Clot resolution checks
  • Comfortable pain relief
  • Early movement encouragement

In-Hospital Healing

1–3 days
  • Catheter site monitoring
  • Anticoagulant therapy
  • Light activity support
  • Medication guidance

Early At-Home Phase

1–2 weeks
  • Follow-up ultrasound
  • Gradual activity increase
  • Compression therapy
  • Ongoing anticoagulation

Complete Recovery

  • Resume normal activities
  • Driving approval
  • Long-term clot prevention
  • Regular vascular checkups
